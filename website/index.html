Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about trading, security, fees, and verification on EthioSwap.

EthioSwap is a peer-to-peer (P2P) escrow platform that allows individuals in Ethiopia to buy and sell USD ($) directly from each other using local payment methods like CBE, Telebirr, and others. When a trade starts, the seller's USD is automatically locked in escrow. Once the buyer sends the fiat payment and the seller confirms receipt, the USD is released to the buyer.

Yes. To ensure a safe, fraud-free environment and comply with AML standards, all users must complete KYC before they can initiate any trades. You will need to upload your National ID or Passport (front and back) along with a live camera selfie. Our admins review submissions quickly to authorize accounts.

When a buyer responds to a sell offer, the seller's USD balance is moved into EthioSwap's secure Escrow vault. The seller cannot cancel the trade while in escrow. The buyer is given 15 minutes to transfer the fiat money to the seller. Once paid, the buyer marks it as paid. The seller then verifies and releases the escrow. If any issue arises, either party can open a dispute.

If a buyer claims they paid but the seller refuses to release, a dispute can be opened. Once active, a certified EthioSwap admin will step into the trade chat. Both users must provide verified screenshots or PDF receipts of the transaction. The admin will verify the logs and release the USD to the buyer or refund to the seller based on audit evidence.

EthioSwap charges a highly competitive, transparent commission fee on every completed trade. The commission rate is set by the platform administrator (typically 1.0%–5.0%) and is shown clearly before you start a trade. Fees are deducted only upon successful trade completion.
